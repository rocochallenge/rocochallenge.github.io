The RoCo (Robotic Collaborative) Assembly Challenge focuses on evaluating Physical AI agents across four core dimensions:

  • Physics Understanding: Grasping object properties, spatial relationships, and real-world dynamics.
  • Dexterous Manipulation: Handling objects with high precision and control.
  • Long-Horizon Reasoning: Reasoning about multi-step tasks, managing dependencies, and recovering from failures.
  • Generalization: Adapting learned skills to novel environments with robust flexibility.
RoCo puts these abilities to the ultimate test: assembly. Our world is built from parts. A robot that can master assembly doesn't just demonstrate high-level intelligence, it bridges the gap from theoretical capabilities to genuine, real-world utility.

Tasks

RoCo adopts two benchmarking tasks:

  • Industrial Board Assembly requires participants to assemble a task board by performing industrial level operations, such as connector insertion, wire routing, screw tightening, and battery assembly, etc.
  • Brick Assembly requires participants to construct customized structures using a set of LEGO bricks.

Industrial Assembly in Simulation

Choose Your Track

RoCo adopts three tracks based on the 2 benchmarking tasks. Teams will compete in one of three tracks this year:

  • Industrial Board Assembly with Grippers
  • Industrial Board Assembly with Dexterous Hands
  • Brick Assembly with Grippers

How to Participate

The challenge is divided into a Simulation Phase and an Onsite Phase.

  • Register: Fill out the Sign-Up Form with your track preference. We will reach out to confirm your placement.
  • Submit (By June 8): Submit your solution proposal. Including simulation results is highly recommended to strengthen your application!
  • Compete Onsite (September 2026): The teams selected from the simulation phase will be invited to the onsite finals at IROS 2026 in Pittsburgh, USA.
