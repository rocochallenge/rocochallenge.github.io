What is RoCo?

The RoCo (Robotic Collaborative) Assembly Challenge focuses on evaluating Physical AI agents across four core dimensions:

  • Physics Understanding: Grasping object properties, spatial relationships, and real-world dynamics.
  • Dexterous Manipulation: Handling objects with high precision and control.
  • Long-Horizon Reasoning: Reasoning about multi-step tasks, managing dependencies, and recovering from failures.
  • Generalization: Adapting learned skills to novel tasks and environments with robust flexibility.
RoCo puts these abilities to the ultimate test: assembly. Our world is built from parts. A robot that can master assembly doesn't just demonstrate high-level intelligence, it bridges the gap from theoretical capabilities to genuine, real-world utility.

Tasks

The 2nd RoCo Challenge adopts two benchmarking tasks:

  • Industrial Board Assembly requires participants to assemble a task board by performing industrial level operations, such as connector insertion, wire routing, screw tightening, and battery assembly, etc. More details can be found in the Industrial Board Assembly Overview.
  • Brick Assembly requires participants to construct customized structures using a set of LEGO bricks. More details can be found in the Brick Assembly Overview.

How to Participate

The challenge is divided into a Simulation Phase and an Onsite Phase.

  • Step 1 - Initial Registration Form: Fill out the Sign-Up Form with your task preference. We will reach out to confirm your placement.
  • Step 2 - Official Competition Proposal: After completing Step 1, submit your proposal through the official Competition Proposal form. Including simulation results is highly recommended to strengthen your application!
  • Compete in Simulation (Now – August): Develop and submit simulation results throughout the simulation competition period.
  • Compete Onsite (September 2026): Onsite finalists announced on August 31 will be invited to the onsite finals at IROS 2026 in Pittsburgh, USA.
Note: Teams must complete both of the following steps, and Step 1 is a prerequisite for Step 2.
